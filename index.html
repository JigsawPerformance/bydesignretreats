A note from Jane

The idea had never once occurred to me — until someone showed me it was possible.

In 2010 I left my employee job to become a contractor. I was done with the politics, done with career paths that didn't interest me, done with the slow grind of other people's agendas. I wanted out — and contracting felt like freedom.

For the first two years, I worked more hours than I ever had as an employee.

It wasn't until my boys were growing up that I felt it — that specific lump in the throat that tells you something matters.

I'd been searching for summer camps for them. Wonderful outdoor ones, a world away from hockey arenas. And realized, somewhere in the scrolling, that I didn't want to drop them off.

I wanted those summers with them.

So we started looking. We researched regions, compared tent trailers, scrolled through vacation rentals, and eventually wandered into properties for sale. That's when we found a cabin on Lake Okanagan. The kind of place that makes the decision for you. We bought it before I had a plan. Before I'd asked anyone's permission. In an industry where two consecutive weeks off was considered career limiting. I had just purchased a summer.

What pushed me over the edge was a conversation with a fellow contractor. She was taking the whole summer off. 'I have horses,' she said, as if it were the most obvious thing in the world.

I have kids.

I asked my boss. A long pause. Then, carefully: 'Will you come back in the fall?'

What I hadn't understood until that moment was simple: as a contractor, my value was in what I delivered, not when I showed up. The project was winding down. Starting the next one in September rather than June wasn't a problem — it was just a conversation I'd never thought to have.

The choice had been mine the entire time. I just hadn't known it.

We kept that cabin for fourteen years. Fourteen summers on the lake with my boys. It was the best thing we ever did for our family — and none of it would have happened if someone hadn't shown me, casually and without fanfare, that a different choice was simply an option.

I had the power all along.
Like Dorothy and her ruby slippers.

That's what I hope this retreat does for you.

— Jane Iannacone Retreat Founder

Before you ask

Your questions, answered.

Here are the things most women want to know before they take the next step.

Will I have my own room?

Yes — every woman has her own private room. If you're attending with a friend and would prefer to share, a double occupancy option is available, which also comes with a cost saving. Either way, your accommodation is fully included in the investment.

What kind of places will we be staying?

We stay in two carefully chosen properties — one near the Lisbon coast and one further into the Portuguese landscape. Depending on what best serves the experience, these may take the form of a boutique hotel, a farm stay, or an eco-luxury resort. What they share is character, comfort, and a genuine connection to the place and the people who live there.

This is where Lystra's philosophy comes to life. Through Qmooniti, every property is chosen not just for how it looks, but for what it represents — places that honour the local culture, support the surrounding community, and reflect Portugal as it actually lives rather than as it's packaged for visitors.

Is it just walking, or are there other experiences?

Walking is the spine of the experience — but the days are shaped by much more than that. We begin in Lisbon with a guided exploration of the city: its history, its neighbourhoods, its particular way of moving through time. Along the way, cultural encounters are woven into each day — with local makers, historians, and guides who bring the places we move through to life. Some evenings unfold over a private chef dinner featuring regional ingredients; others take us to local restaurants where Portugal reveals itself slowly.

Again, this is where Lystra's curation makes the difference. Every experience is chosen to be immersive rather than touristic — encounters that leave you with a felt sense of Portugal, not just a photograph of it.

How much walking is involved? Do I need to be fit?

Five days include guided walks. These are not performance walks — the pace is unhurried and routes are chosen for atmosphere and landscape, not difficulty. If a particular day doesn't feel right for you, there's no obligation to join every walk. This experience is shaped around you, not the other way around.

Can I come on my own?

Most women do. The pre-trip gathering — held before departure — means you'll already know the women you're travelling with before you land in Lisbon. You arrive as part of a group, not as a stranger.

How many women will be in the group?

The group is intentionally small — enough to feel like a genuine circle, small enough that everyone knows everyone by the end of day one. Exact numbers are confirmed with the full details shared to waitlist members.

What are the dates?

Final dates are confirmed with waitlist members once the itinerary is locked. What we can tell you now: the retreat takes place in early October 2026, and the group will be home by October 8th at the latest. If you're thinking about flights, that's your planning anchor.

What is the investment?

This is a fully curated, all-inclusive experience — private rooms, chef-prepared meals, local expert guides, and private transfers throughout, with Lystra on the ground with you every step of the way. Investment details, including the dual occupancy option, are shared first with waitlist members.
